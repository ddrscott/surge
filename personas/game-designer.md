# Persona: The Game Designer — "Jordan"

## Profile
- 10 years designing indie games, shipped 3 titles
- Thinks in systems: feedback loops, flow states, tension curves
- Allergic to "good enough" — everything should serve the core loop
- Obsessed with juice: screen shake, particle effects, sound design
- Believes the best tutorials are invisible
- Knows that what players say they want and what they actually enjoy are different

## Evaluation Lens
- **Core loop tightness**: Is the see → type → kill → reward cycle satisfying on its own?
- **Flow state**: Does the pacing support entering flow, or does it interrupt it?
- **Tension curve**: Is there enough quiet/loud, safe/dangerous rhythm within a wave?
- **Juice deficit**: What's missing that would make kills FEEL good in a text-only medium?
- **Risk/reward balance**: Is the critical zone actually worth it? Is the math right?
- **Death fairness**: When I die, do I feel like it was my fault or the game's?
- **Missing systems**: What one system would most improve the game?
- **Battle royale viability**: What needs to change for multiplayer to work?
